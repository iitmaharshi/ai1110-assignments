\item A die is thrown twice. What is the probability that:\\
(i) 5 will not come up either time?\\
(ii) 5 will come up at least once?\\

\solution \\
5 appearing on the dice is treated as success with probability $p = \frac{1}{6}$ and 5 not appearing on the dice is treated as failure with probability $q = \frac{5}{6}$\\
Here we have done 2 trials, so \textbf{n = 2}.\\
Let $X \in \lbrace 0,1,2 \rbrace$ be the random variable representing the number of $5_{s}$ appeared after both the trials\\
X = 0 : zero $5_{s}$\\
X = 1 : exactly one 5\\
X = 2 : exactly two $5_{s}$\\
By bernoulli distribution,
\begin{align}
P\brak{X=r} = {n\choose r} \times p^{r} \times q^{n-r}
\end{align}
Probability that no 5 appears\brak{say P_{1}} :
\begin{align}
P_{1}&= P\brak{X=0}\\
&= {n\choose 0} \times p^0 \times q^2\\
&= \brak{\frac{5}{6}}^2\\
&= \frac{25}{36}
\end{align}
Probability that atleast one 5 appears\brak{say P_{2}} :
\begin{align}
P_{2}&= P\brak{X=1} + P\brak{X=2}\\
&= {n\choose 1} \times p^1 \times q^1 + {n\choose 2} \times p^2 \times q^0\\
&= \frac{10}{36} + \frac{1}{36}\\
&= \frac{11}{36}
\end{align}
\textbf{ALTERNATE METHOD:}
we know that,since the events are exhaustive and exclusive
\begin{align}
P_{1} + P_{2} &= 1\\
\implies P_{2} &= 1 - P_{1}\\
\implies P_{2} &= 1 - \frac{25}{36}\\
\implies P_{2} &= \frac{11}{36}
\end{align}
