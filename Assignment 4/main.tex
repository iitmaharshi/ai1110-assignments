\item A die is thrown twice. What is the probability that:\\
(i) 5 will not come up either time?\\
(ii) 5 will come up at least once?\\

\solution \\

Let X$\in\lbrace1,2,3,4,5,6\rbrace$ be a random variable representing the number obtained on the first throw of the dice.\\
Similarly, Let Y$\in\lbrace1,2,3,4,5,6\rbrace$ be a random variable representing the number obtained on the second throw of the dice.\\
For our case X is independent of Y.
\begin{table}[h!]
\centering
\caption{}
\input{tables/table.tex}
\label{Table1}
\end{table}

(i)\\
we are required to find P\brak{\brak{X != 5}\cap\brak{Y != 5}}\\
we know by Conditional probability that:\\
\begin{align}
P\brak{A\cap B} &= P(A|B)\times P(B)\\
\\
Let: P &= P\brak{\brak{X!=5}\cap\brak{Y!=5}}\\ \label{eq1}
\implies P &= P\brak{X!=5} \times P\brak{Y!=5}\\
\implies P &= \brak{1 - P\brak{X = 5}}\brak{1 - P\brak{Y = 5}}
\end{align}
Inputting values in \eqref{eq1} from the table:
\begin{align}
P &= \brak{1 - \frac{1}{6}}\brak{1 - \frac{1}{6}}\\
\implies P &= \frac{25}{36}
\end{align}
(ii)\\
Since the event M(say) = "not getting 5 on either throw of dice" and event N(say) = "getting atleast one 5" are exhaustive events:\\
\begin{align}
P\brak{M} + P\brak{N} &= 1\\
\implies P\brak{N} &= 1 - P\brak{M}\\
\therefore P\brak{N} &= \frac{11}{36}
\end{align}
\end{enumerate}
